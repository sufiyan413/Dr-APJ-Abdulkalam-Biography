Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Former President of India

Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to India's space and missile programs, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." He played a pivotal role in the successful launch of India's first satellite, Aryabhata, and the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Known for his humility and dedication, Dr. Kalam was a beloved figure in India and an inspiration to millions. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
